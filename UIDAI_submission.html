Problem Statement

Aadhaar is a continuity-dependent public infrastructure. While enrolment volumes may appear stable, Aadhaar usability can silently break due to delayed biometric updates, lifecycle transitions (especially among children), or fragile service delivery capacity. These failures often surface only when citizens experience authentication denial or exclusion from welfare services.

This project asks: Where is Aadhaar continuity likely to break in the next 6–12 months, and can the system absorb that risk before citizens are affected?

Analytical Approach

  • Service Resilience Index (SRI) – Measures the system’s ability to absorb demand shocks using volatility, acceleration, and peak stress.
  • Continuity Break Risk (CBR) – Estimates risk of Aadhaar becoming unusable due to delayed biometric updates and lifecycle transitions.
  • Intervention Priority Ranking (IPR) – Combines SRI and CBR into a ranked, decision-grade action list.
This system is predictive, interpretable, and designed for governance use — not automation.

Decision Readiness

This system is designed to integrate into monthly UIDAI operational reviews. Outputs are intended to support:

  • UIDAI HQ strategic planning
  • State Registrar capacity planning
  • District-level preventive outreach
If no action is taken during elevated risk windows, continuity failures compound, increasing the probability of citizen exclusion.

Governance & Ethics

  • Aggregated data only — no individual identification
  • No automated enforcement or denial
  • Decision-support, not decision replacement
  • Transparent assumptions and thresholds
